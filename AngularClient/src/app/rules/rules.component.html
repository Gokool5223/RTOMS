RTO Rules and Regulations

Imported vehicles are considered to be a part of foreign trade, therefore, Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Directorate General of Foreign Trade, Export-Import Policy Department along with Motor Act are all a part of the importation procedure. There are some requirements which as per the RTO office, must be met when importing a vehicle:

  • The speedometer of the vehicle has to comply with the measuring units used in India.
  • The imported car should be a right-hand drive.
  • The photometry of the vehicle should suit ‘Keep Left’ traffic.
  • All the formalities of the Motor Act 1988 must be fulfilled.

Let us also understand that there are two categories of Imported Vehicles-New Imported Vehicles and Used Imported Vehicles. Thus, you need to know the Guide for Imported Vehicles.

Though the above-mentioned rules apply to both the categories, there are some guidelines for each:

The RTO Rules and Regulations for New Imported Vehicles

  • The vehicle has neither been manufactured nor assembled in India.
  • Before it's importation to India, the vehicle has neither been sold nor leased.
  • Prior to importation, the vehicle has not been registered in any other country.

Also, whether it is a dealer or an importer who is importing the vehicle, should have a valid certificate of compliance as ordered by the Central Motor Vehicle Rules (CMVR), 1989, rule number 126. He should also submit the proof of compliance as per rule 126A, within 6 months of the import.

The RTO Rules and Regulations for Used Imported Vehicles

  • The used vehicle must not be more than three years old.
  • The vehicle must comply with the provisions laid down by the Motor Vehicle Act,1988.

When importing the vehicle, the importer or the dealer has to submit a certificate that is issued by a testing agency that the used vehicle is being imported into India. The certificate must also state that the vehicle has been tested and complies to the original homologation certificate.

The RTO Rules and Regulations for Registration

The imported vehicle has to receive RTO Registration number from the nearest RTO office. For the first 7 days, the vehicle receives a Temporary RTO Registration number, after which the owner has to apply for a permanent RTO number. The RTO online form can be downloaded from the official website.
